How do I make my browser-based tests deterministic? — QUESTION_TITLE_MARKER

I have a Playwright suite of about 800 tests. Roughly 1-2% fail on any given CI run, but the same tests rarely fail twice. My team is exhausted from re-running. What are the highest-leverage things I can do to convert the suite from "mostly passes" to "always passes or really failing"?

The tactical mitigations matter, but the structural issue is almost always test isolation. Before you fix the clock or the RNG, audit how much state leaks between tests — cookies, localStorage, in-memory caches in your test server, shared user rows in the database. Most "flaky" suites are actually order-dependent suites that happen to pass in the common ordering.

Concretely: run your suite with --shuffle or equivalent and watch what happens. If the failure rate goes up, that's your work list, in priority order. If it stays flat, you have a non-determinism problem (clock, RNG, network), which is the easier fix. ANSWER_2_BODY_MARKER
